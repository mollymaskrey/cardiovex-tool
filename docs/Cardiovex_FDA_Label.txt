CARDIOVEX®
 (cardiovexaban) tablets, for oral use
 WARNING: BLEEDING RISK
 
CARDIOVEX increases the risk of bleeding and can cause significant and sometimes
fatal bleeding. Do not use CARDIOVEX in patients with active pathological bleeding or a
 history of intracranial hemorrhage.
 
Most bleeding events with CARDIOVEX are gastrointestinal. Intracranial hemorrhage
rates are low and comparable to placebo (0.4% vs 0.3%). Monitor patients for signs and
 symptoms of bleeding. Discontinue CARDIOVEX in patients with active bleeding.
1 INDICATIONS AND USAGE
CARDIOVEX is a dual P2Y12/PAR-1 platelet inhibitor indicated to reduce the risk of thrombotic cardiovascular events
in adult patients with established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD) and elevated residual risk despite
guideline-directed medical therapy.
Specific Patient Population:
 Documented ASCVD: Prior myocardial infarction (>30 days), prior ischemic stroke (>90 days), or symptomatic
peripheral arterial disease with objective evidence
 Elevated residual risk: 10-year ASCVD risk score ≥15% OR ≥2 high-risk features (diabetes with target organ damage,
chronic kidney disease stage 3+, or recurrent events within 24 months)
 On guideline-directed medical therapy: Stable antiplatelet (aspirin or clopidogrel), high-intensity statin with LDL <100
mg/dL, and blood pressure control
2 DOSAGE AND ADMINISTRATION
2.1 Recommended Dosage
The recommended dosage of CARDIOVEX is 150 mg orally once daily with or without food.
Continue background antiplatelet therapy (aspirin 75-100 mg daily OR clopidogrel 75 mg daily, not both) and statin
therapy.
2.2 Switching from Other Antiplatelet Agents
When switching from clopidogrel or ticagrelor, initiate CARDIOVEX 24 hours after the last dose. When switching from
prasugrel, initiate CARDIOVEX 48 hours after the last dose.
3 DOSAGE FORMS AND STRENGTHS
CARDIOVEX tablets: 150 mg, round, blue, film-coated tablets debossed with 'CVX 150' on one side
4 CONTRAINDICATIONS
CARDIOVEX is contraindicated in patients with:
 Active pathological bleeding (e.g., peptic ulcer, intracranial hemorrhage)


 History of intracranial hemorrhage
 Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C)
 Hypersensitivity to cardiovexaban or any component of CARDIOVEX


5 WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS
5.1 Increased Risk of Bleeding
CARDIOVEX increases the risk of bleeding. In the SHIELD-1 trial, TIMI major bleeding occurred in 3.8% of
CARDIOVEX-treated patients versus 2.1% with placebo (HR 1.82, 95% CI 1.39-2.38, p<0.001) over a median of 36
months. Most bleeding events (70%) were gastrointestinal.
Intracranial hemorrhage rates were low and balanced: 0.4% (CARDIOVEX) versus 0.3% (placebo), HR 1.23, 95% CI
0.59-2.58, p=0.58. This finding was confirmed in SHIELD-2 (0.4% vs 0.3%, HR 1.36, p=0.44) and the pooled analysis
of 16,048 patients (HR 1.29, p=0.35).
5.2 Discontinuation for Surgery and Invasive Procedures
Discontinue CARDIOVEX 5-7 days prior to elective surgery or invasive procedures with a major risk of bleeding.
Bridging antiplatelet therapy is generally not recommended. Resume CARDIOVEX when hemostasis has been
established.
5.3 Concomitant Use with Other Antiplatelet Agents
CARDIOVEX was studied with background aspirin OR clopidogrel monotherapy. Concomitant use with dual
antiplatelet therapy (aspirin plus P2Y12 inhibitor) has not been studied and increases bleeding risk. Do not use
CARDIOVEX with dual antiplatelet therapy beyond 12 months post-ACS or post-PCI.
5.4 Gastrointestinal Bleeding Risk Factors
Patients with prior gastrointestinal bleeding, active peptic ulcer disease, inflammatory bowel disease, or concurrent use
of medications that increase bleeding risk (anticoagulants, chronic NSAIDs, SSRIs/SNRIs, corticosteroids) are at
increased risk for GI bleeding. Consider proton pump inhibitor therapy in patients at high risk.

6.2 Non-Bleeding Adverse Reactions
Non-bleeding adverse reactions reported in ≥2% of CARDIOVEX-treated patients and more frequently than placebo:

7 DRUG INTERACTIONS
7.1 Strong CYP3A Inhibitors
Avoid concomitant use of CARDIOVEX with strong CYP3A inhibitors (e.g., ketoconazole, itraconazole, clarithromycin,
ritonavir). These agents increase cardiovexaban exposure and bleeding risk.
7.2 Strong CYP3A Inducers
Avoid concomitant use with strong CYP3A inducers (e.g., rifampin, carbamazepine, phenytoin, St. John's wort). These
agents may reduce cardiovexaban exposure and efficacy.
7.3 Anticoagulants and Antiplatelet Agents
Concomitant use with oral anticoagulants, direct thrombin inhibitors, or Factor Xa inhibitors increases bleeding risk.
Avoid concomitant use unless benefit outweighs risk. CARDIOVEX was studied with background single antiplatelet
therapy (aspirin OR clopidogrel), not dual antiplatelet therapy.
8 USE IN SPECIFIC POPULATIONS
8.1 Pregnancy
Risk Summary: There are no adequate and well-controlled studies of CARDIOVEX in pregnant women. Animal
reproduction studies have not been conducted. It is not known whether CARDIOVEX can cause fetal harm when
administered to a pregnant woman.
8.2 Lactation
Risk Summary: It is not known whether cardiovexaban is present in human milk. Because many drugs are present in
human milk and because of the potential for serious adverse reactions in nursing infants, a decision should be made
whether to discontinue nursing or to discontinue the drug.
8.4 Pediatric Use
Safety and effectiveness in pediatric patients have not been established.
8.5 Geriatric Use
Of the 8,024 patients in SHIELD-1 and SHIELD-2 who received CARDIOVEX, 3,850 (48%) were ≥65 years and 1,284
(16%) were ≥75 years. No overall differences in safety or effectiveness were observed between these patients and
younger patients.
8.6 Renal Impairment
Mild to moderate renal impairment (eGFR 30-89 mL/min/1.73 m2) does not require dose adjustment. CARDIOVEX has
not been studied in patients with severe renal impairment (eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73 m2) or on dialysis. Use with caution
in these populations.
8.7 Hepatic Impairment
CARDIOVEX is contraindicated in patients with severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C). No dosage adjustment is
needed for patients with mild or moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh A or B).


10 OVERDOSAGE
There is no known specific antidote to CARDIOVEX. Treatment of overdose should follow general supportive
measures. Monitor for signs of bleeding and manage bleeding events with standard supportive care including
transfusion support as needed. Due to the antiplatelet mechanism, dialysis is unlikely to be effective. Cardiovexaban's
antiplatelet effects dissipate within 3-5 days of drug discontinuation based on pharmacodynamic studies.
11 DESCRIPTION
CARDIOVEX (cardiovexaban) is a dual-pathway antiplatelet agent that selectively and reversibly inhibits both the
P2Y12 receptor (blocking ADP-mediated platelet activation) and the protease-activated receptor-1 (PAR-1, blocking
thrombin-mediated platelet activation).
The chemical name is (synthetic structure not disclosed for fictional compound). The molecular formula is
C24H27N5O3S with a molecular weight of 465.57 g/mol. Cardiovexaban is a white to off-white crystalline powder,
slightly soluble in water.
Each CARDIOVEX tablet contains 150 mg of cardiovexaban and the following inactive ingredients: microcrystalline
cellulose, croscarmellose sodium, hypromellose, magnesium stearate, titanium dioxide, polyethylene glycol, and
FD&C; Blue #2 aluminum lake.
12 CLINICAL PHARMACOLOGY
12.1 Mechanism of Action
Cardiovexaban and its active metabolite inhibit platelet aggregation through dual pathways. P2Y12 receptor
antagonism blocks ADP-induced platelet activation and aggregation. PAR-1 receptor antagonism blocks
thrombin-induced platelet activation. Dual inhibition provides more complete suppression of platelet activation than
single-pathway strategies.
12.2 Pharmacodynamics
In phase 2 studies, cardiovexaban 150 mg once daily demonstrated superior platelet inhibition compared with
clopidogrel 75 mg daily as measured by ADP-induced and thrombin-induced platelet aggregation assays. Maximal
platelet inhibition is achieved within 2 hours of dosing. Pharmacodynamic effects are sustained over 24 hours,
consistent with once-daily dosing.
12.3 Pharmacokinetics
Absorption: Cardiovexaban is rapidly absorbed after oral administration with peak plasma concentrations (Cmax)
occurring at 1-2 hours post-dose. Absolute bioavailability is approximately 75%. Food does not significantly affect
exposure.
Distribution: Cardiovexaban is highly protein-bound (>95%) to albumin and alpha-1-acid glycoprotein. Volume of
distribution at steady state is approximately 165 L.
Metabolism: Cardiovexaban is primarily metabolized by CYP3A4 to an active metabolite. Both parent compound and
metabolite contribute to pharmacological activity.
Excretion: Cardiovexaban is eliminated primarily via hepatic metabolism and biliary excretion. Terminal half-life is
approximately 8-10 hours, supporting once-daily dosing.


14 CLINICAL STUDIES
14.1 Secondary Prevention in High-Risk ASCVD (SHIELD-1 and SHIELD-2)
The efficacy of CARDIOVEX was established in two randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials: SHIELD-1
(N=8,206) and SHIELD-2 (N=7,842). Both trials enrolled patients with established ASCVD (prior MI, prior ischemic
stroke, or symptomatic PAD) and elevated residual risk (ASCVD risk score ≥15% or ≥2 high-risk features) despite
guideline-directed medical therapy including antiplatelet agent (aspirin or clopidogrel) and high-intensity statin.
The primary endpoint was time to first MACCE (cardiovascular death, myocardial infarction, stroke, or urgent coronary
revascularization). Median follow-up was 36 months in both trials.

17 PATIENT COUNSELING INFORMATION
Advise the patient to read the FDA-approved patient labeling (Medication Guide).
Bleeding Risk
Inform patients that CARDIOVEX increases the risk of bleeding and to report any unanticipated, prolonged, or
excessive bleeding, or blood in stool or urine. Advise patients to inform physicians and dentists that they are taking
CARDIOVEX before any surgery or dental procedure is scheduled and before any new drug is taken.
Adherence to Therapy
Instruct patients to take CARDIOVEX exactly as prescribed and not to discontinue without first discussing with their
healthcare provider. Inform patients that premature discontinuation increases the risk of cardiovascular events.
Missed Doses
If a dose is missed, instruct patients to take CARDIOVEX at the next scheduled time and not to double the dose.
